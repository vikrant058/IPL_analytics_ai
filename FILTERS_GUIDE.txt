1. MATCH PHASE / OVERS (POWERPLAY, MIDDLE, DEATH)
================================================================================

WHAT IS IT?
  Divides a 20-over T20 match into three distinct phases based on overs played.

CATEGORIES:
  ‚Ä¢ POWERPLAY (Overs 1-6)
    - First 6 overs of each innings
    - Fielding restrictions apply (max 2 fielders outside 30-yard circle)
    - Aggressive batting expected, bowlers under pressure
    - Example: "Rohit's powerplay statistics"

  ‚Ä¢ MIDDLE OVERS (Overs 6-15.6)
    - The consolidation phase of the innings
    - Mid-game momentum building
    - Mix of aggressive and careful cricket
    - Example: "Kohli's average in middle overs"

  ‚Ä¢ DEATH OVERS (Overs 16-20)
    - Final 5 overs of an innings (also called "slog overs")
    - High-risk aggressive batting
    - Bowlers try to restrict runs with yorkers and variations
    - Example: "Bumrah's death bowling economy"

HOW IT AFFECTS PERFORMANCE:
  ‚úì Powerplay: High SR required, more dot balls acceptable
  ‚úì Middle: Acceleration phase, balance between aggression & survival
  ‚úì Death: Maximum SR, high-risk shots, swinging bat mentality

USE CASES:
  "Dhoni in death overs 2015-2020"           ‚Üí Captain Dhoni's specific strength
  "Compare Jasprit vs Bhuvneshwar in death"  ‚Üí Head-to-head death bowling
  "Gayle's powerplay performance"            ‚Üí Opens with aggressive approach

2. BOWLER TYPE (PACE, SPIN, HAND ORIENTATION)
================================================================================

WHAT IS IT?
  Categorizes bowlers based on bowling style and hand orientation.
  Different bowlers provide different challenges to batsmen.

CATEGORIES:

  A. BY STYLE:
    ‚Ä¢ PACE / FAST BOWLERS
      - Speed: 130+ km/h
      - Uses: Pace, bounce, line/length accuracy
      - Examples: Bumrah, Siraj, Cummins
      - Use query: "Kohli vs pace bowlers"

    ‚Ä¢ SPIN BOWLERS
      - Medium pace: 80-120 km/h with rotation
      - Uses: Turn, flight, variation
      - Includes: Off-spinners, Leg-spinners
      - Examples: Ashwin, Varun, Bishnoi
      - Use query: "Dhoni vs spinners performance"

  B. BY HAND ORIENTATION:
    ‚Ä¢ LEFT-ARM BOWLERS
      - Unique angles and movement
      - Challenging for some batsmen (especially right-handers)
      - Examples: Starc, Kumble
      - Use query: "Rohit against left-arm bowlers"

    ‚Ä¢ RIGHT-ARM BOWLERS (majority of bowlers)
      - Standard approach for most
      - Use query: "Kohli vs right-arm pace"

  C. BY SPECIFIC SPIN TYPE (ADVANCED):
    ‚Ä¢ OFF-SPINNER
      - Turns away from right-handed batter
      - Examples: Ashwin, Sundar
      - Use query: "Kohli vs off-spinners"

    ‚Ä¢ LEG-SPINNER
      - Turns towards right-handed batter
      - More unpredictable, higher risk for bowler
      - Examples: Yuzvendra Chahal, Ish Sodhi
      - Use query: "Virat vs leg-spinners" (historically struggles)

HOW IT AFFECTS PERFORMANCE:
  ‚úì Some batsmen naturally struggle against spin/pace
  ‚úì Left vs Right creates angle advantages
  ‚úì Experience level varies with different bowling styles

USE CASES:
  "Kohli average vs leg-spinners"                    ‚Üí Known weakness
  "Bumrah vs right-arm pace bowlers"                 ‚Üí Unusual comparison
  "How does Dhoni play left-arm spinners?"           ‚Üí Role-specific analysis
  "Hardik Pandya performance against off-spinners"   ‚Üí Specialization analysis

3. MATCH SITUATION (BATTING FIRST vs CHASING)
================================================================================

WHAT IS IT? ‚≠ê MOST IMPORTANT FOR 2026 IMPLEMENTATION
  Divides matches into two distinct contexts based on which team bats first.
  Affects strategy, pressure, and psychology dramatically.

CATEGORIES:

  A. BATTING FIRST (SETTING THE TOTAL)
    ‚Ä¢ Team bats first, tries to score maximum runs
    ‚Ä¢ Uncertainty: Don't know target until 2nd innings
    ‚Ä¢ Strategy: Aggressive, score quickly, build platform
    ‚Ä¢ Pressure: Medium-high (need good total)
    ‚Ä¢ Example: "Rohit's batting first average: 52, Chasing average: 41"

  B. CHASING (PURSUING THE TARGET)
    ‚Ä¢ Team knows exact target needed to win
    ‚Ä¢ Total required varies (100 to 200+)
    ‚Ä¢ Psychological pressure varies greatly

    SUB-TYPES:
    
    1. COMFORTABLE CHASE (RRR < 8 runs/over)
       - Easy target, less risky approach possible
       - Wicket preservation more important
       - Example: "Chasing 140 in 20 overs"
       - SR required: 100-120 acceptable
       - Use: "Kohli in easy chases" or "Comfortable chase performance"

    2. NORMAL CHASE (RRR 8-10 runs/over)
       - Standard T20 pace needed
       - Balanced approach: Aggression + Safety
       - Example: "Chasing 160 in 20 overs"
       - SR required: 130-150 needed
       - Use: "Ishan Kishan's performance in normal chases"

    3. PRESSURE CHASE (RRR > 10 runs/over)
       - High-risk, high-reward situation
       - Requires aggressive approach from start
       - Wickets lost early = almost impossible
       - Example: "Chasing 180 in 20 overs"
       - SR required: 160+ needed
       - Use: "Bumrah economy in pressure chase situations"
       
       SUB-SUB-TYPES:
       ‚Ä¢ SUPER OVER EQUIVALENT (RRR > 15)
         - Cricket's most exciting moments
         - All-or-nothing mentality
         - Example: "Dhoni in super-over situations"

HOW IT AFFECTS PERFORMANCE:
  ‚úì Batting First: Less pressure initially, but need strong finish
  ‚úì Chasing (Comfortable): More cautious, lower SR acceptable
  ‚úì Chasing (Pressure): Aggressive from ball 1, higher SR needed
  ‚úì Psychological factors: Chase failures > Batting failures

REAL-WORLD EXAMPLES:

  Query: "Kohli's chasing performance"
  ‚Üí Returns stats from matches where team batted 2nd
  
  Query: "Bumrah in pressure chases"
  ‚Üí Shows bowling economy when RRR needed was > 10 runs/over
  
  Query: "Dhoni's comfortable chase batting"
  ‚Üí Returns stats from chases where RRR < 8 (low pressure)
  
  Query: "Rohit's batting first vs chasing average"
  ‚Üí Compares his two different batting approaches
  
  Query: "IPL - chasing success rate in 2024"
  ‚Üí Shows how often teams won while chasing

5. BATTER ROLE / POSITION (OPENING, MIDDLE ORDER, LOWER ORDER)
================================================================================

WHAT IS IT?
  Categorizes batting position (where in the order a batter plays).
  Different positions have different demands and match-up patterns.

CATEGORIES:

  ‚Ä¢ OPENER (#1, #2 position)
    - Faces first bowls (fresh bowlers, new ball)
    - Aggressive approach expected
    - Building foundation for innings
    - Examples: Rohit, Gill, Ishan
    - Use: "Rohit as opener statistics" or "Gill's opening partnership avg"

  ‚Ä¢ MIDDLE ORDER (#3, #4, #5 position)
    - Balance between aggression and stability
    - Often elevated when team loses early wickets
    - More flexible approach needed
    - Examples: Kohli (usually #3), Surya, Rana
    - Use: "Kohli's middle-order performance vs opening"

  ‚Ä¢ LOWER ORDER (#6, #7, #8+ position)
    - Specialist batters or bowler-batters
    - Finishing roles or rescue situations
    - Much higher SR expected (160+)
    - Examples: Hardik, Pollard, Pant
    - Use: "Hardik's lower-order average vs middle-order"

HOW IT AFFECTS PERFORMANCE:
  ‚úì Different positions = Different stats and approaches
  ‚úì Some players perform better at specific positions
  ‚úì Position changes can indicate form or team strategy
  ‚úì Lower order needs higher SR to be valuable

USE CASES:
  "Kohli as opener vs his usual #3 position"           ‚Üí Position analysis
  "Pant in middle order vs lower order SR comparison"  ‚Üí Role versatility
  "Hardik's average when batting #3 vs #6"             ‚Üí Position importance
  "Opening pair statistics: Rohit + Ishan"             ‚Üí Partnership analysis

6. VERSUS CONDITIONS (vs PACE, vs SPIN, HOME, AWAY)
================================================================================

WHAT IS IT?
  Contextualizes performance against specific bowling types or venues.

CATEGORIES:

  A. BOWLING TYPE OPPOSITION:
    ‚Ä¢ VS PACE BOWLERS
      - Performance against fast bowling (130+ km/h)
      - Use: "Rohit average vs pace bowlers"
      - Notable: Some batsmen struggle with express pace

    ‚Ä¢ VS SPIN BOWLERS
      - Performance against spinners (both off & leg)
      - Use: "Kohli average vs spin bowlers"
      - Notable: Indian batsmen usually strong; overseas weak

  B. VENUE CONTEXT:
    ‚Ä¢ HOME GAMES
      - Matches at team's home stadium
      - Familiar pitch, crowd support
      - Conditions known
      - Use: "Mumbai Indians' home record in powerplay"
      - Example: Bumrah's economy at Wankhede vs elsewhere

    ‚Ä¢ AWAY GAMES
      - Matches at opponent's stadium
      - Unfamiliar pitch, hostile crowd
      - Travel fatigue factor
      - Use: "Royals away vs home average"
      - Example: "Dhoni in away chases"

HOW IT AFFECTS PERFORMANCE:
  ‚úì Home advantage: ~5-10% performance boost typical
  ‚úì Away teams often chase more defensively
  ‚úì Batsmen's approach differs (pace vs spin)
  ‚úì Bowlers' effectiveness varies with conditions

USE CASES:
  "Kohli average vs pace vs spin"                      ‚Üí Weakness identification
  "Bumrah economy at home vs away"                     ‚Üí Venue impact on bowling
  "Dhoni's home and away chasing averages"             ‚Üí Location psychology
  "Compare player performance Wankhede vs Eden"        ‚Üí Specific venue analysis
